What "Free" Really Means (The Hidden Catches)

Most "free" poll makers come with strings attached. A tool might be free to use but require signup, limit your response count, restrict features, or add their branding to your poll. Before you commit to any tool, understand what "free" actually includes.

True free tools let you create unlimited polls, collect unlimited responses, and share without a paywall. But they're rare. Most free tiers sacrifice something: features, response limits, branding control, or data export capabilities.

When evaluating a free poll maker, ask these questions:

  • ✓Do I need to sign up?
  • ✓Can I create unlimited polls?
  • ✓Are there response limits?
  • ✓Can I remove the tool's branding?
  • ✓Can I export or download results?
  • ✓What features are behind paywalls?

Frequently Asked Questions

What does "free" really mean for poll makers? +
"Free" can mean no signup required, no credit card, no vote limits, and no hidden paywalls. Not all free tools offer all four. Some require signup but no payment. Others allow unlimited votes but restrict features.
Which free poll maker is fastest to use? +
VoteGenerator and StrawPoll are the fastest—both let you create and share a poll in under 60 seconds with zero signup required. Google Forms is slower because it requires a Google account and takes a few minutes to set up.
Can I remove branding from a free poll? +
Most free tools show their branding on polls by default, and removing it requires a paid plan — including VoteGenerator. StrawPoll and Google Forms are exceptions where branding is minimal or absent even on free tiers.
Which free poll maker supports the most poll types? +
Google Forms and Typeform offer the widest variety of question types (15+). For basic polling, VoteGenerator, StrawPoll, and Google Forms all handle the common types well.
Do I need to pay to see poll results? +
With most free tools, results are always free to view and share. Some charge for advanced features like real-time analytics, CSV export, or detailed reports—but viewing basic results is always free.
Are there response limits on free plans? +
Yes, and they vary dramatically. VoteGenerator allows 100 responses per poll (with 3 active at once). StrawPoll and Google Forms are unlimited. Typeform allows only 10 responses per month total. Poll Everywhere caps at 25 per poll. Mentimeter limits you to 50 participants per month cumulative. SurveyMonkey allows 25 per survey.
What's the difference between a poll and a survey? +
Polls are typically quick, with 1–3 questions and simple response options. Surveys are longer, multi-question forms designed for detailed feedback. All tools here support both, though some (like SurveyMonkey) are optimized for surveys specifically.
