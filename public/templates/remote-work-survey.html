The Remote Work Reality

What Remote Employees Actually Struggle With

Remote work offers significant advantages in productivity and work-life balance — but those benefits aren't evenly distributed. Employees with inadequate home setups, unreliable connectivity, or an inability to switch off experience remote work very differently from those with dedicated workspace and good technology. This survey segments those experiences so you can target support where it's actually needed rather than applying blanket policy.

🔇
Social isolation
Loss of informal connection, missing hallway conversations, feeling out of the loop on team culture.
📡
Tech and connectivity
Unreliable internet, poor home setup, inadequate equipment for video calls and collaborative work.
⚖️
Work-life boundary
Inability to switch off, longer working hours, workspace bleeding into living space.
📋
Information gaps
Missing context that would have been picked up organically in an office. Async communication gaps.

Question 9 (biggest remote work challenge) reveals which of these categories dominates at your organisation. Each requires a different intervention. Social isolation needs structured social touchpoints; tech challenges need equipment stipends; boundary issues need working hours norms; information gaps need better async documentation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this remote work survey template free?
Yes. Free forever with no signup from you or your remote team. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses — better for large distributed organisations.
What questions should a remote work survey include?
The most useful work from home survey questions cover: overall satisfaction, home workspace quality, internet and tech reliability, team connection, collaboration tools effectiveness, productivity vs in-office, work-life balance, hybrid vs fully remote preference, and biggest challenge. This template covers all nine.
How often should I run a remote work survey?
Run quarterly for remote-first teams, as conditions evolve quickly. Run when making policy changes — shifting to hybrid, changing office requirements, or updating remote work stipends. Run with new hires at 60-90 days to catch early friction before it becomes attrition.
How do I improve remote team connection?
Question 4 (team connection score) paired with Question 9 (biggest challenge) reveals the specific type of isolation. Social isolation needs informal channels and virtual coffee; informational gaps need better async documentation; professional isolation needs visible career development. Each requires a different fix.
What is the difference between a remote work survey and a pulse survey?
A remote work survey focuses specifically on the distributed work experience — workspace, tools, connectivity, isolation. A pulse survey measures weekly morale broadly. Use this template annually or when making remote policy changes; use our Weekly Pulse template for ongoing morale monitoring.
Does this work for hybrid teams?
Yes. Question 8 specifically asks about hybrid vs fully remote preferences, making this equally valuable for hybrid teams assessing whether their current setup is working. All questions are editable — you can adjust framing for part-time remote employees before sharing.
