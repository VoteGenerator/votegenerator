FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this membership satisfaction survey template free?
Yes. Free forever with no signup from you or your members. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses and renewal cycle tracking analytics.
When should I run a membership satisfaction survey?
60–90 days before renewal periods — giving you time to address concerns before members decide. Also run at membership milestones: first 30 days, 6 months, and annual anniversaries to track the member lifecycle.
What is the most important membership survey question?
Question 8 (renewal intent) is most commercially important. However, Q4 (community and belonging) is most predictive of that renewal — members with social connections cancel at 60% lower rates. Fix community to protect renewal.
Can I use this for a gym or fitness centre?
Yes. Q2 covers equipment and facilities. Q4 (community) is the underrated retention driver for gyms — members who feel part of a fitness community cancel at 60% lower rates than those who don't.
Can I use this for a professional or trade association?
Yes. Adjust terminology — replace 'facilities' with 'resources and benefits', 'community' with 'professional network', and 'programmes' with 'events and CPD'. Value for money and renewal intent questions are equally critical for associations.
How do I reduce membership churn with survey data?
Identify the dimension with the largest gap between your highest and lowest scores — that is your primary retention lever. For gyms: facilities and value. For associations: community and communication. Fix the lowest dimension before renewal opens.
Can this work for online membership communities?
Yes. Adjust Q2 to cover content and platform quality, Q4 to cover member interaction, and Q6 to cover newsletter quality. All 9 dimensions apply to virtual membership with minor terminology adjustments.
How many responses do I need for meaningful data?
20% of your active membership for statistically meaningful data. For a 500-member organisation, that's 100 responses — covered by the free plan. The open text question (Q9) is most actionable at any sample size.
