How We Tested These Tools

We created identical surveys in each platform to test setup time, ease of use, question types available, customisation options, and the actual limits you hit on the free tier.

Testing Criteria
  • No-signup vs signup required: Can you create and share without an account?
  • Question types: Multiple choice, open-ended, rating scales, matrices, ranking
  • Response limits: Unlimited responses or capped, and at what number?
  • Export options: Can you download results as CSV or Excel on the free plan?
  • Customisation: Branding, colours, conditional logic
  • Mobile responsiveness: Does it work on phones?
  • Time to launch: Minutes from signup to sharing a live survey

1. VoteGenerator

1
VoteGenerator
Best for no-signup speed
Best for Speed

Price: Free · Signup: None required · Free responses: 100 per poll · Active polls: 3 at a time

VoteGenerator is purpose-built for creating quick surveys and polls with zero friction. You land on the site, start typing your question, and share a link or QR code in under a minute. The free tier provides all poll types and real-time results. CSV export, advanced analytics, and branding removal require a paid plan.

Pros
  • Zero signup friction
  • QR code generation for instant mobile sharing
  • Real-time response tracking
  • All 8 poll types on free tier
  • Clean, intuitive interface
Cons
  • 100 responses per poll on free tier
  • CSV export requires paid plan
  • 3 active polls at a time (free)

Verdict

If you need to launch a survey in 30 seconds with zero friction, VoteGenerator is unbeatable. The no-signup approach makes it ideal for event feedback, classroom polls, or quick customer tests. The honest trade-off is the 100-response cap and paid-only CSV export, but for basic feedback collection those constraints rarely matter.

2. Google Forms

2
Google Forms
Best for structured surveys
Solid Choice

Price: Free with Google account · Signup: Requires Google account

Google Forms is the default choice for businesses and teams already in Google's ecosystem. You get unlimited responses, full CSV export, and integration with Google Sheets for real-time analysis.

Pros
  • Unlimited responses, no caps
  • Full CSV export free
  • Google Sheets integration
  • 10+ question types
  • Team collaboration
Cons
  • Requires a Google account
  • Limited visual customisation
  • Interface feels dated
  • Not ideal for branded surveys
Verdict

For internal surveys and team feedback, Google Forms is reliable and trusted. It is especially useful if you already have a Google account and want to analyse results in Sheets. For customer-facing surveys where branding matters, you will feel the limits quickly.

3. SurveyMonkey

3
SurveyMonkey
Free tier has serious limits
Limited Free

Price: Free (limited) · Paid from $25/month · Signup: Required · Free responses: 25 per survey

SurveyMonkey is built to convert free users to paid plans. The free tier caps you at 25 responses per survey and removes CSV export entirely. Response limits reset when you start a new survey, but that means losing your data continuity.

Pros
  • Wide range of professional templates
  • Advanced question types
  • Good testing ground before upgrading
  • Mobile responsive
Cons
  • Only 25 responses per survey free
  • No CSV export on free plan
  • Requires signup and email validation
  • Paid plan needed for real use
Verdict

The 25-response cap makes SurveyMonkey's free tier impractical for anything beyond testing. Better to use Google Forms or VoteGenerator first, then upgrade to SurveyMonkey's paid plans if you need their advanced analytics and templates.

4. Typeform

4
Typeform
Beautiful forms, tight free tier
Limited Free

Price: Free (limited) · Paid from $25/month · Signup: Required · Free responses: 10 per month across all forms

Typeform is visually stunning and justifiably popular for branded surveys. But the free tier is extremely restricted. You get just 10 total responses per month shared across every form in your account. That is not per survey; it is 10 total for the entire month.

Pros
  • Exceptional visual design
  • Customisable themes and branding
  • Good question variety
  • Conversational survey flow
Cons
  • Only 10 responses per month free
  • No CSV export on free plan
  • Requires signup
  • Expensive paid plans
Verdict

Use Typeform's free tier only as a design reference or prototype. Ten responses per month makes it unusable for real surveys. If you love Typeform's look, the paid plan is worth it. Otherwise, Google Forms or Tally offer better free-tier value.

5. Jotform

5
Jotform
Good for form builders
Decent Option

Price: Free · Paid from $34/month · Signup: Required · Free tier: Limited monthly submissions, 5 forms max

Jotform is primarily a form builder that handles surveys well. The free tier has monthly submission limits, extensive templates, and conditional logic available without upgrading. CSV export requires a paid plan.

Pros
  • Many pre-built templates
  • Conditional logic on free tier
  • Good question variety
  • Mobile responsive
Cons
  • CSV export is paid only
  • Requires signup
  • Free tier limited to 5 forms
  • Interface feels crowded
Verdict

Jotform's free tier is better than SurveyMonkey or Typeform for form building. If you do not need to export results immediately, it works. The CSV export paywall makes data analysis frustrating on the free plan.

6. Microsoft Forms

6
Microsoft Forms
Best for Office 365 users
Solid for MS Users

Price: Free with Microsoft account · Signup: Requires Microsoft / Office account

If your organisation uses Microsoft 365, Forms integrates seamlessly with your existing tools. Unlimited responses and Excel integration make it a strong free option, provided you already have an Office account.

Pros
  • Unlimited responses
  • Excel integration for analysis
  • CSV export available
  • Built-in analytics
Cons
  • Requires Microsoft account
  • Limited customisation
  • No conditional logic
  • Less intuitive than Google Forms
Verdict

If you are already in the Microsoft ecosystem with Office 365, use Forms. Otherwise, Google Forms is more accessible. For pure survey needs, neither beats VoteGenerator's no-signup simplicity.

7. Zoho Survey

7
Zoho Survey
Limited free tier
Skip Unless on Zoho

Price: Free (limited) · Paid from $25/month · Signup: Required · Free responses: 25 per survey

Zoho Survey caters to organisations using other Zoho products. The free tier's 25-response limit is one of the lowest we tested. Without Zoho CRM or similar, there is little reason to choose this over Google Forms.

Pros
  • Zoho ecosystem integration
  • Decent question variety
  • Good templates
Cons
  • Only 25 responses free
  • CSV export is paid only
  • Requires signup
  • Not as polished as competitors
Verdict

Skip the free tier unless you are already using Zoho CRM or other Zoho products. The 25-response cap is too restrictive for meaningful survey work. Better alternatives are available at no cost.

8. SurveySparrow

8
SurveySparrow
NPS specialist, limited free
Niche Use Only

Price: Free (limited) · Paid from $99/month · Signup: Required · Free responses: 25 per survey

SurveySparrow specialises in NPS and customer satisfaction surveys. The design is polished and mobile-first. But the 25-response free cap and expensive paid plans make it hard to recommend unless you specifically need its NPS features.

Pros
  • Excellent for NPS and CSAT
  • Beautiful survey design
  • Mobile-first approach
Cons
  • Only 25 responses free
  • No CSV export free
  • Paid plans start at $99/month
  • Designed for specialised use cases
Verdict

If you specifically need NPS surveys and have budget, SurveySparrow is professional and polished. For general-purpose surveys, the free tier is too limiting and paid plans are too expensive for most small teams.

9. Tally

9
Tally
Minimalist form building
Underrated Pick

Price: Free · Signup: Required (free account) · Free responses: Unlimited

Tally is a newer entrant focusing on clean, simple form building. The free tier is genuinely comprehensive: unlimited responses, CSV export included, and no ads. If you want a no-fuss experience and are willing to create a free account, Tally is worth a look.

Pros
  • Unlimited responses on free
  • CSV export included free
  • Clean, minimalist interface
  • Conditional logic available
  • No ads on free plan
Cons
  • Requires a free signup
  • Fewer templates than Google Forms
  • Smaller community
  • Limited customisation and branding
Verdict

Tally is an underrated option. If you prefer minimalism, want free exports, and are fine creating an account, it sits nicely between VoteGenerator (no signup) and Google Forms (more features). Worth trying before defaulting to Google.

10. LimeSurvey

10
LimeSurvey
Open-source enterprise option
Technical Users Only

Price: Free (self-hosted) · Cloud from $99/month · Signup: Required (for cloud)

LimeSurvey is open-source survey software. The cloud version competes with enterprise solutions, but self-hosting is completely free if you have the technical resources. It is a mature, well-documented platform.

Pros
  • Self-hosting is completely free
  • Unlimited responses and exports
  • Advanced branching logic
  • Full customisation
Cons
  • Self-hosting requires technical setup
  • Steep learning curve
  • Cloud version is expensive
  • Interface feels dated
Verdict

LimeSurvey is best for organisations with development resources to self-host or that need enterprise features. For most small businesses, the setup complexity does not justify it when Google Forms or VoteGenerator are available at zero friction.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is VoteGenerator really free with no signup required? +
Yes. VoteGenerator requires no account, email, or credit card. You create a survey and share it immediately. The free tier allows 100 responses per poll and up to 3 active polls at once. CSV export and advanced analytics require a paid plan.
Which free survey maker is best for businesses? +
It depends on your use case. Google Forms is the safest default if you have a Google account and need unlimited responses with free CSV export. For quick surveys without signup friction, VoteGenerator is ideal. For advanced logic and professional exports, SurveyMonkey's paid tier offers more features but costs money.
Can I export survey results from free survey makers? +
Most free survey makers restrict CSV exports to paid plans. Google Forms and Microsoft Forms allow CSV exports on the free tier, as does Tally. VoteGenerator, SurveyMonkey, Typeform, Jotform, SurveySparrow, and Zoho Survey all require paid plans for data export.
What are the response limits for free survey makers? +
Response limits vary dramatically. Google Forms, Microsoft Forms, and Tally have unlimited responses. VoteGenerator allows 100 responses per poll (with 3 active polls). SurveyMonkey limits you to 25 responses per survey. Typeform allows only 10 responses per month across all your forms. Zoho Survey and SurveySparrow cap at 25 responses per survey. Always verify the current terms before launching a survey you expect to scale.
Do free survey makers work on mobile? +
All the tools we tested are mobile-responsive for respondents. Desktop creation interfaces vary in usability on smaller screens, but the survey-taking experience is consistently good across platforms.
Can I brand a survey with my company logo and colours? +
Branding customisation is almost always a paid feature. Typeform and VoteGenerator allow branding removal and custom colours on paid plans. Tally offers basic colour customisation free. Google Forms and Microsoft Forms have very minimal branding options. Most other tools restrict custom branding to paid tiers.
How long does it take to create a survey? +
VoteGenerator is fastest at under 1 minute with no account required. Google Forms and Tally typically take 3 to 5 minutes. SurveySparrow, Typeform, and others with more design options take longer if you customise. For basic surveys, 5 to 10 minutes is typical across most platforms.
