The Data That Drives Renewal Decisions

Most HR teams negotiate benefits renewals based on utilisation data from providers — but utilisation tells you what people used, not what they value. An employee who never claimed on the vision plan didn't necessarily not value it — they may have just been healthy that year. This benefits satisfaction survey tells you what people actually care about keeping, cutting, or adding.

Run this survey 2–3 months before your renewal date. That's enough time to use the data in negotiations with your broker, present a business case to leadership for restructuring, and communicate changes to employees before they take effect.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this employee benefits survey template free?
Yes. Free forever with no signup from you or your employees. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses — better for large organisations surveying all employees simultaneously.
What questions should an employee benefits survey include?
The most useful benefits surveys cover: most valued benefits, overall satisfaction, health coverage, PTO adequacy, retirement plan quality, remote work support, unmet benefit needs, and whether benefits influence the decision to stay. This template covers all nine dimensions.
How often should I run an employee benefits survey?
Run a comprehensive benefits survey annually, 2-3 months before your renewal period so you have data for broker negotiations. Run a shorter pulse check after benefits changes to measure adoption and satisfaction. New hires should complete it after 30-60 days once they've experienced the full package.
How do I use benefits survey data to negotiate with providers?
Benefits providers respond to data. If 60% of employees don't value the current vision plan but 80% want mental health coverage, that's your case for restructuring. Present specific percentages to your broker rather than anecdotal feedback. Pro accounts export to CSV for formal reporting.
Should the benefits survey be anonymous?
Yes. Anonymous surveys produce significantly more honest answers on sensitive topics like compensation and benefits satisfaction. Employees may underreport dissatisfaction if they worry their answers affect their relationship with HR. VoteGenerator stores no names or email addresses by default.
Can I add questions specific to my company's benefits?
Yes. All 9 questions are editable. Free accounts support up to 10 questions — enough to add one company-specific question. Pro ($19/month) allows up to 25 questions, useful for organisations with complex benefits programmes.
