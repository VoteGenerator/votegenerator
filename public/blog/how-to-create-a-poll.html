Why Online Polls Beat Email Chains

Email chains are slow, messy, and easy to lose in your inbox. When you're trying to gather quick feedback—whether it's picking a meeting time, deciding on a team lunch spot, or getting votes on an important decision—a traditional email becomes cumbersome fast.

Online polls solve this problem. They're instant, visual, and give you results in real-time. A shareable link works across email, text, Slack, and social media. Your respondents don't need accounts. No endless back-and-forth. Just clear, organized data that updates as people vote.

Step 1: Pick Your Poll Type

VoteGenerator offers 8 different poll types. The type you choose depends on what you're asking and how you want people to respond.

What to Do

Go to votegenerator.com/create and browse the available poll types. Each type has a clear icon and description. Multiple choice is the most common starting point—people select one option from a list. But you might also want a yes/no poll, a ranking poll, or an RSVP poll depending on your use case.

💡Pro Tip: If you're unsure which type fits your question, start with multiple choice. It works for 80% of polling scenarios.

Step 2: Write Your Question & Add Options

Your poll's success depends on clear wording. People should understand what you're asking in one read. Avoid jargon and keep your question short—ideally one sentence.

What to Do

Enter your poll question in the text field. Then add your response options. For a multiple-choice poll, you need at least 2 options but can add as many as you want (though 4–5 is ideal for clarity). Each option should be distinct and non-overlapping.

✍️Example: Instead of "When should we have the meeting?" (too vague), write "Which day works best for next week's team meeting?" and list your specific days.

Step 3: Customize Settings

This is where you fine-tune your poll to match your needs. Most of these options are optional, but they give you control over how people interact with your poll.

Key Options
  • Poll Duration: Set an end date/time, or leave it open indefinitely
  • Vote Limits: Allow one vote per person, or multiple votes per person
  • Visibility: Keep results hidden until voting ends, or show them live
  • Colors & Branding: Change the poll colors to match your brand (optional)
  • Add Images: Attach images to each option for visual polls
🔒Best Practice: If you want authentic results and don't want people's choices to influence others, hide results until voting ends.

Step 4: Share Your Link & Collect Votes

Once your poll is created, VoteGenerator generates a unique, shareable link. This is your poll's public URL. Anyone with the link can vote—no sign-up required on their end either.

How to Share
  • Email: Copy the link into an email. A short intro like "Quick poll: Which dessert should we serve at the event?" is all you need.
  • Slack/Teams: Paste the link into a channel with a brief message
  • Text/SMS: Works great for quick, informal polls
  • Social Media: Share on Twitter, LinkedIn, or Facebook
  • Embed on Website: VoteGenerator generates embed code for your website or blog

Watch your results update in real-time as votes come in. You can monitor progress from your poll's dashboard.

Tips for Getting More Responses

Creating a poll is easy. Getting people to actually vote takes a bit of strategy. Here are proven tactics to boost participation:

1
Use a Clear, Curious Subject Line

In email, your subject line decides whether people open the message. Instead of "Team Poll," try "Which coffee shop should we use for our next offsite?" Curiosity drives clicks.

2
Keep Options Simple

Too many choices paralyze people. Stick to 3–5 options. Voters move faster, and results are clearer.

3
Set a Deadline

Open-ended polls have lower response rates. A specific deadline ("Vote by Friday") creates urgency and encourages immediate action.

4
Make It Personal

Explain why you're asking. "We're picking our team's summer activity—your vote matters!" beats a generic poll announcement.

5
Send Reminders

A follow-up message 1–2 days before your deadline catches people who missed the first message. A simple "Reminder: Vote here" works.

6
Share Results Afterward

Close the loop. Tell people what won and what you're doing with the results. This builds trust and encourages future participation.
