The Care Journey

What Each Question Measures

Patient satisfaction isn't a single feeling — it's the sum of every touchpoint across the care journey. A patient who loved the doctor but waited 45 minutes and dealt with an unfriendly receptionist will not leave a 5-star review. This template covers all four stages of the experience your healthcare patient feedback needs to capture.

Patient loyalty is built in the first three stages but measured in the fourth. A practice with excellent clinical outcomes but poor wait time management and unfriendly front desk staff will see it in their NPS and their Google Reviews — usually at the same time. This patient satisfaction survey template gives you the data before it becomes a public problem.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this patient satisfaction survey template free?
Yes. Free forever with no signup from you or your patients. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses — better for busy practices collecting patient feedback continuously.
What questions should a patient satisfaction survey include?
The most valuable patient satisfaction survey questions cover: overall care experience, provider communication and listening, wait time satisfaction, staff friendliness, facility cleanliness, likelihood to recommend (NPS), return visit intent, and open feedback. This template covers all eight dimensions.
When should I send a patient satisfaction survey?
Send within 24 hours of the appointment while the experience is fresh. Text or email surveys immediately post-visit achieve the highest response rates. Avoid sending more than 48 hours after — patient recall decreases and response rates drop significantly.
Does this patient survey comply with HIPAA?
VoteGenerator collects survey responses anonymously — no names, email addresses, or patient identifiers are stored. However, practices should consult their compliance officer before implementing any patient communication tool to confirm it meets their specific HIPAA requirements.
How do I share this survey with patients?
Share via text post-appointment, in your patient portal, on printed cards at checkout, via QR code in the waiting room, or in your post-visit email. No login required from patients — they click and respond immediately in their browser.
Can I use this for dental or specialist practices?
Yes. Works for any healthcare setting — GP practices, dental offices, specialist clinics, physiotherapy, mental health, and urgent care. All 8 questions are editable. Replace "doctor" with "dentist" or "therapist" before sharing.
How do I use patient feedback to improve care?
Focus on the gap between your highest and lowest dimension scores. If care quality scores 4.6 but wait time scores 2.8, wait time management is your operational priority. The open text question reveals specific patient concerns. Aggregate at least 20 responses before drawing conclusions to avoid outlier bias.
