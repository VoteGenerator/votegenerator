All 9 Canteen Survey Questions

Nine questions covering everything from what ends up on the plate to how long it takes to get it. Question 3 (value for money) is consistently the strongest predictor of whether staff actually use the canteen. Employees who feel the food is overpriced relative to quality stop coming and bring their own lunch, which then reduces revenue per head and makes it harder to justify the catering budget overall.

Question 6 (dietary requirements) is often where canteens lose the trust of a meaningful portion of the workforce. Staff who feel their dietary needs are treated as an afterthought tend to stop using the canteen entirely, and they often say so in the open text. Getting this right can have a bigger positive impact on overall canteen usage than almost any other change.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this canteen survey template free?
Yes. Free forever with no signup. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses and trend tracking across survey cycles.
How often should I run a canteen survey?
Twice a year as a baseline. That gives catering operators enough time to make real changes between surveys. Run an extra pulse after significant menu changes, a new catering contract, or a refurbishment. For larger sites with multiple outlets, consider surveying each outlet separately.
Can I use this survey to evaluate a contract caterer?
Yes. Staff survey results are some of the most useful data for assessing catering contract performance and planning re-tendering. Share aggregated scores alongside any contractual KPIs. If scores are consistently low on specific dimensions, include them as performance improvement requirements in your contract review.
What is the most important canteen survey question?
Value for money (Q3) is consistently the strongest predictor of canteen usage. Staff who feel the food is overpriced relative to quality stop coming, which reduces revenue per head and makes it harder to justify the catering budget. Improving perceived value is often more achievable through better communication about ingredients and sourcing than through price reductions alone.
Should canteen surveys be anonymous?
Yes. Employees are more likely to give honest feedback about quality, hygiene concerns, or value if anonymous. This is especially important for hygiene feedback, where staff may be reluctant to report concerns if they think it could cause problems for individual catering team members.
How does canteen quality connect to return-to-office culture?
Several workplace studies found that food and social spaces are among the top three factors employees cite when choosing whether to come into the office on a given day. Canteen survey data is relevant not just to facilities teams but to HR and leadership thinking about hybrid working and office attendance.
Can this survey be used for university or school catering?
Yes. It works for university refectories, school canteens, hospital staff restaurants, and public sector cafeterias. Adjust the survey title and introduction for your setting. For student-facing catering, consider adding a question on opening hours, which is often the top complaint alongside value for money.
How do I present results to the catering team without creating conflict?
Share results as improvement opportunities rather than performance grades. Frame each low score as a question: what would need to change for staff to rate this higher? Include positive scores alongside critical ones. Share with the catering team before presenting to leadership so they are not blindsided. The open text suggestions are usually the most constructive starting point.
