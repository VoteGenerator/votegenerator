All 8 Delivery Feedback Questions

Eight questions that cover the complete delivery experience from kitchen to doorstep. Question 1 (order accuracy) is the most operationally important: every missing or wrong item drives disproportionate frustration and is the leading cause of negative platform reviews. It is also entirely preventable with better kitchen processes. Question 7 (ordering again) is your commercial health check: a customer who says no here is at risk of switching to a competitor and leaving a public review before they do.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this food delivery survey template free?
Yes. Free forever with no signup. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses and trend tracking across delivery zones and menu changes.
How is this different from a restaurant feedback survey?
A restaurant survey covers the in-person dining experience: ambience, service at the table, and the overall visit. This delivery survey covers the remote order experience: order accuracy, how food travelled, packaging integrity, delivery timing, and courier behaviour. The packaging and order accuracy questions are unique to delivery.
When should I send this survey?
Within 30 minutes of delivery confirmation. Response rates drop sharply after 2 hours because the meal experience fades. The ideal trigger is automatic: send via SMS or email as soon as the order status updates to delivered.
What is order accuracy and why does it drive reviews?
Order accuracy measures whether the customer received exactly what they ordered: no missing items, no wrong items, no unrequested substitutions. A single missing item generates disproportionate frustration because the customer cannot quickly resolve it the way they could in a restaurant. It is consistently the top driver of negative platform reviews on Uber Eats, Deliveroo, and Just Eat.
Can ghost kitchens and dark kitchens use this survey?
Yes. Ghost kitchens rely entirely on delivery feedback to understand their customer experience since there is no in-person interaction. Order accuracy (Q1), packaging quality (Q3), and food quality on arrival (Q2) are the three dimensions that ghost kitchens control entirely.
How do I collect feedback if I use a third-party platform?
Third-party platforms have their own review systems which you cannot control. Running your own survey gives you private, actionable data the platform does not share with you. It also gives early warning of issues before they turn into public negative reviews. Include a link in any post-order communications you control.
Can I use this for catering and corporate food delivery?
Yes. For corporate catering, adjust Q3 (packaging) to cover bulk packaging and Q5 (courier) to cover the professionalism of the delivery team. The order accuracy question is even more important for catering orders where a missing component can disrupt a meeting or event.
How do delivery reviews affect platform rankings?
On platforms like Deliveroo, Uber Eats, and Just Eat, your ranking is partly determined by your average rating and review volume. Private feedback collection before customers reach the platform review page lets you identify dissatisfied customers and offer to resolve their issue, reducing the chance of a public negative review.
