Property Management Dimensions

What Determines Whether Tenants Stay

Tenant satisfaction and renewal intent are driven by three overlapping factors — not just the physical quality of the property. A beautifully maintained flat with poor communication and slow maintenance response will see high turnover. Understanding which dimension is the priority for your specific portfolio helps you allocate maintenance and management resources effectively.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this tenant satisfaction survey template free?
Yes. Free forever with no signup from you or your tenants. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses for larger property portfolios.
What questions should a tenant satisfaction survey include?
Cover: overall satisfaction, property condition, maintenance responsiveness, communication quality, safety and security, common area quality, value for money, renewal intent, and open feedback. This template covers all nine dimensions.
How often should landlords run tenant satisfaction surveys?
Annually as a baseline. At lease renewal time to capture satisfaction at the most commercially sensitive moment. For larger developments, every six months — tenant satisfaction at 6 months is highly predictive of renewal intent at 12 months.
What are the Tenant Satisfaction Measures (TSMs)?
TSMs are performance indicators required by the Regulator of Social Housing in England since April 2023. This template covers the key perception-based TSM dimensions — satisfaction, maintenance responsiveness, communication, safety, and complaint handling. Housing associations should verify their specific reporting requirements.
How do I use tenant satisfaction data to reduce vacancy rates?
Question 8 (renewal intent) is the most commercially important. Properties with satisfaction above 4.0 see renewal rates 35-45% higher than those below 3.0. Identify the two lowest dimensions and address them before lease renewal conversations begin. Proactive improvement is 5-10× more cost-effective than finding new tenants.
Should tenant surveys be anonymous?
Yes — tenants are reluctant to report genuine dissatisfaction, safety concerns, or maintenance problems if responses are identifiable. Particularly on sensitive issues like neighbour disputes or landlord communication problems, anonymity is essential for honest feedback.
Should I share tenant survey results with tenants?
Yes — and you should. Sharing aggregated results and the specific improvements you're making significantly increases response rates in future surveys. Landlords who share results and act on them report 25-30% higher renewal rates than those who survey without sharing outcomes.
What should I do if tenants report safety concerns?
Safety concerns identified in tenant surveys should be triaged immediately — regardless of anonymity. If aggregate results indicate safety dissatisfaction, conduct a physical inspection and communicate proactively with all tenants. Never delay acting on safety data because it was collected anonymously.
