FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this meeting feedback survey template free?
Yes. Free forever, no signup from you or your team. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses and timeline analytics to track meeting quality week over week.
When should I send a meeting feedback survey?
Send within 30 minutes of the meeting ending while it's still fresh. For recurring meetings, run the survey once per quarter rather than every session to avoid survey fatigue. The most valuable time to survey is before you put a new meeting on a recurring calendar slot.
What questions should a meeting feedback survey include?
The most useful meeting feedback questions cover: overall rating, time value, agenda clarity, goal achievement, time management, recurrence recommendation, and one improvement suggestion. This template covers all seven.
Should meeting feedback be anonymous?
Anonymous feedback is more honest, particularly from junior team members rating meetings run by senior leaders. VoteGenerator surveys are anonymous by default — no names or email addresses stored. This gives attendees the confidence to say "this could have been an email" without fear of consequences.
How do I stop unnecessary recurring meetings?
Question 6 ("Should this meeting recur?") with "Could have been an email" as an explicit option gives you the data to make that case. When 40%+ of attendees say a meeting could have been an email, that's the number you bring to leadership to justify cancelling or restructuring it.
Can I use this for virtual and remote meetings?
Yes. Works for any meeting format — in-person, video call, hybrid. For virtual meetings, consider adding a question about platform experience. All questions are fully editable after clicking Use This Template.
