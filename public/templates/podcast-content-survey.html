Frequently Asked Questions

Is this podcast feedback survey template free?
Yes. Free forever with no signup. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses and trend tracking across survey cycles.
When should podcasters run an audience survey?
Quarterly surveys show how your show is evolving and whether it still matches listener interests. Many podcasters run an annual listener survey as a standalone episode. Run a pulse after significant format changes like moving to video, changing episode length, or bringing on a new host.
Can this survey be used for YouTube and video creators?
Yes. Adjust the audio quality question to cover video and production quality. The topic relevance and format preference questions are equally important for video creators because the YouTube algorithm responds to consistent topic focus and audience retention signals.
What is the most important podcast survey question?
Question 2 (topic relevance) is most strategically important. Creators often drift from their original niche, and audience surveys are the early warning system for when that drift has gone too far. The open text question (Q9) is the most creatively useful: listeners often name specific topics, guests, or formats the creator has not considered.
How do surveys and analytics work together?
Analytics tell you what listeners do: download counts, where they drop off, which episodes performed best. Surveys tell you why: what topics matter, what they wished were different, and what would make them recommend. Combining both is more diagnostic than either source alone.
Can newsletter and written content creators use this?
Yes. Adjust the framing: replace 'episode' with 'newsletter', 'audio quality' with 'readability and formatting', and 'guest quality' with 'quality of referenced sources'. The format preference question is particularly useful for newsletters.
How do I share the survey with my audience?
Mention the survey in the episode itself with a link in the show notes. Share on social media. Include in your email list. For YouTube, a community post or pinned comment works well. Many podcasters get the best response by offering something in return: a chance to vote on a future topic or a name mention in an upcoming episode.
How many responses do I need for meaningful data?
For a podcast with 1,000 or fewer regular listeners, 50 to 100 responses gives you meaningful directional data. A 5 to 10 percent response rate from your regular audience is typical. The open text question tends to be the most valuable regardless of sample size: even 20 detailed responses can reshape your content strategy.
