All 8 Class Feedback Questions

Eight questions covering the complete class experience from the moment it starts to how members feel walking out. Question 2 (instructor quality) is the most commercially important metric: members will return to average facilities for an exceptional instructor and leave exceptional facilities for a poor one. It covers technical delivery, motivation, cueing, and how well the instructor read the room.

Question 7 (book again) is your retention health check. Members who attend classes regularly are 3 to 4 times more likely to renew their membership. A low score here is an early warning that a member is drifting toward cancellation, often weeks before they take action.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this gym fitness class survey template free?
Yes. Free forever with no signup. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses and per-instructor tracking.
How is this different from a gym membership survey?
A membership survey measures the overall experience: access, facilities, equipment availability, and overall value. This class survey focuses on a single session: instructor delivery, class structure, difficulty, and atmosphere. Run class surveys per session and membership surveys quarterly or annually.
What is the most important question in a fitness class survey?
Question 2 (instructor quality) is the strongest predictor of rebooking and retention. Members will return to average facilities for an exceptional instructor. Low instructor scores are your most actionable data because they point directly to coaching and development needs.
When should gyms send this survey?
Immediately after the class or within 2 hours while the physical experience is still fresh. Send via SMS or push notification from your booking app as soon as the class ends. Response rates drop significantly after 4 hours.
Can boutique studios and personal trainers use this?
Yes. Works for boutique studios (spin, HIIT, yoga, Pilates, barre), large gym group exercise classes, and personal training sessions. For personal training, adjust the framing to cover the one-to-one session. The instructor quality questions become even more important when the entire experience depends on a single person.
Can I use this to evaluate individual instructors?
Yes, with appropriate management. Require at least 15 to 20 responses per instructor before drawing conclusions. Present scores as development conversations rather than performance grades. Pair scores with anonymised open-text quotes for specific, actionable feedback.
What is class difficulty calibration and why does it matter?
Difficulty calibration measures whether the class was pitched at the right level. A class that is too easy feels like a waste of time. One that is too hard discourages beginners from returning. Consistently low scores here suggest the class description does not match reality, creating mismatched expectations.
Can this survey work for virtual and online fitness classes?
Yes. For virtual classes, adjust the facility question to cover the online experience: stream quality, connection reliability, and whether the virtual format felt engaging. The instructor quality and difficulty questions are equally relevant, and arguably more important, since the instructor cannot demonstrate physically alongside participants.
