FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this conference feedback survey template free?
Yes. Free forever with no signup from you or your attendees. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses — better for large conferences with hundreds of attendees.
What questions should a conference feedback survey include?
Effective post-conference surveys cover: overall event rating, speaker quality, session content relevance, networking value, venue and logistics, value for money, NPS, return intent, and one open improvement suggestion. This template covers all nine dimensions.
When should I send the conference feedback survey?
Send during the final session or within 2 hours of closing — response rates drop by 50% after 24 hours. For multi-day conferences, consider a short mid-event pulse on Day 1 to allow real-time adjustments, followed by the full survey on the final day.
How do I get attendees to complete the feedback survey?
Mention the survey verbally from the stage during the closing session with the link on-screen. Drop the link in the event Slack, app, or WhatsApp group simultaneously. Include a QR code on the final slide. Post-event email within 2 hours. Offering early-bird access to next year's tickets to completers lifts response rates to 70%+.
Does this work for virtual and hybrid events?
Yes. For virtual events, Question 5 (venue and logistics) becomes about platform quality — editable to reference your event platform, video quality, and technical accessibility. For hybrid events, run separate surveys for in-person and virtual attendees as the experience differs significantly.
How do I use conference feedback data to improve next year's event?
Focus on the lowest-scoring dimension, the networking score (most common attendee frustration), and the open text responses (Question 9) which reveal specific improvements attendees want. Present the data to your programme committee before speaker selection opens for the next event.
What is a good NPS score for a conference?
Conference NPS typically ranges from +20 to +70. Above +40 is considered strong — meaning a clear majority of attendees would actively recommend the event to peers. Below +20 suggests the event is not creating strong enough value to drive organic word-of-mouth, the primary growth mechanism for most annual conferences.
Can I use this for seminars, webinars, and trade shows?
Yes. Works for any event format. For webinars, adjust Question 5 to cover platform and technical quality. For trade shows, add a question about booth or exhibitor experience. All 9 questions are editable to reflect your specific event format.
Can I track conference quality year over year?
Yes. Run the same survey at each annual event. The trend across years — particularly NPS, return intent, and content relevance — shows whether your event is growing in perceived value. Pro ($19/month) includes multi-event timeline analytics.
Should conference feedback surveys be anonymous?
Yes — anonymous surveys produce more honest feedback about specific speakers or organisational decisions. Attendees who are industry peers of the speakers may give inflated ratings if responses are identifiable. VoteGenerator stores no names or email addresses by default.
How is this different from the event app's built-in feedback tool?
Event app feedback tools are typically locked to the app platform, limited in question customisation, and don't provide downloadable results. This template works as a standalone URL or QR code, requires no app installation from attendees, and gives you full control over questions and live aggregated results — including CSV export on Pro.
Can I use this for internal company conferences?
Yes. Works for company all-hands, leadership conferences, team off-sites, and internal training events. For internal events, the NPS question measures internal reputation rather than external word-of-mouth — still a useful signal for event organisers. All questions are fully editable.
