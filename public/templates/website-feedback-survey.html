When to Show It

4 High-Intent Trigger Moments

The timing of your website feedback survey matters as much as the questions. Showing it on page load annoys visitors before they've experienced anything. These four moments deliver 3–5× higher response rates and significantly more useful answers — because users have actually experienced what you're asking them to rate.

🚪
Exit Intent
Trigger as the cursor moves toward the browser tab or close button. Catches visitors before they leave with unresolved frustrations.
✅
Post-Completion
After purchase, signup, or form submission — when satisfaction is highest and the experience is freshest.
⏱️
Timed Trigger
After 45–90 seconds on a page — indicating genuine engagement rather than accidental landing.
📉
High Exit Rate Pages
Deploy on pages identified via analytics as having unexplained high exit rates. Let visitors tell you why they're leaving.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this website feedback survey template free?
Yes. Free forever with no signup from you or your visitors. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses and response timeline analytics for ongoing UX monitoring.
What questions should a website feedback survey include?
Cover: ease of finding information, design clarity, trustworthiness, task completion, mobile experience, NPS, return intent, and one open improvement suggestion. This template covers all eight UX dimensions.
When should I show a website feedback survey?
Trigger on exit intent, after task completion, after 45–90 seconds of engagement, or on high-exit-rate pages identified in analytics. Avoid showing on first page load — wait until users have experienced the site.
How is a website feedback survey different from a usability test?
Usability testing observes specific users completing defined tasks in a controlled environment. A website feedback survey collects quantitative feedback from real visitors across all their varied goals — faster to deploy and scales to far more users. Both are valuable.
Can I use this for an e-commerce website?
Yes. For e-commerce, show the survey on the order confirmation page post-purchase. The trustworthiness question (Q3) is particularly valuable — perceived credibility directly affects conversion rate for first-time buyers.
What is a good NPS score for a website?
Technology: +35 to +55. E-commerce: +25 to +45. B2B SaaS: +20 to +50. Consumer services: +10 to +30. More useful than benchmarks is tracking your own NPS trend — consistent improvement matters more than the absolute score.
How do I use website feedback data to improve conversion rate?
Question 5 — whether the visitor completed their goal — combined with the open text response tells you exactly what prevented conversion. Fix the most commonly cited barriers first. This is conversion rate optimisation research you'd pay thousands for in a formal UX audit.
How do website UX survey results affect SEO?
Indirectly but significantly. Better UX reduces bounce rate and increases dwell time — signals Google uses as quality indicators. High trustworthiness scores predict lower bounce rates. Fixing "can't find information" responses typically reduces bounce rate on landing pages. UX and SEO are converging.
