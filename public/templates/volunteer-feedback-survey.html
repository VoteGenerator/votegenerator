Why Volunteers Leave

What This Survey Actually Measures

Volunteer retention is one of the hardest operational challenges for nonprofits and charities. The average volunteer retention rate is just 65% — meaning one in three volunteers who give their time don't return. The reasons are rarely about disliking the cause. They're almost always about the experience of volunteering itself.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this volunteer feedback survey template free?
Yes. Free forever with no signup from you or your volunteers. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses — better for large organisations with regular volunteer programmes.
What questions should a volunteer experience survey include?
The most effective volunteer feedback surveys cover: overall experience, communication quality, sense of meaningful contribution, role preparation and training, team welcome and inclusion, likelihood to return, NPS (recommend to a friend), and open improvement feedback. This template covers all eight dimensions.
When should I send a volunteer feedback survey?
Send within 24 hours of the volunteer shift or event while the experience is fresh. For ongoing volunteer programmes, survey every 3-6 months to avoid survey fatigue. For one-time event volunteers, send immediately after the event wraps.
How do I retain volunteers using survey data?
Question 3 (meaningful contribution) is the most predictive of retention. Volunteers who score this 3 or below are at high attrition risk — they feel their time isn't making a difference. Address this by communicating impact clearly after every shift: how many meals served, families supported, or funds raised. Make the difference visible.
Can I use this survey for event volunteers?
Yes. Works for any volunteering context — ongoing programme volunteers, one-time event volunteers, corporate volunteering days, charity fundraising, and community projects. All 8 questions are editable. Replace 'volunteer programme' with 'event' or 'day' before sharing.
How is a volunteer survey different from a general satisfaction survey?
Volunteer feedback surveys focus on the specific motivations that drive volunteering — purpose, belonging, meaningful contribution, and personal development. A general satisfaction survey measures service quality. This template captures the emotional dimensions that predict whether a volunteer will return, not just whether they had a 'satisfactory' experience.
Should volunteer feedback surveys be anonymous?
Anonymous surveys produce more honest feedback, particularly about organisational culture. Volunteers in small community organisations who know the coordinator personally may give inflated ratings to avoid awkwardness. VoteGenerator stores no names or email addresses by default.
