Why Feedback Works

What Good Spa Feedback Actually Tells You

Most clients who don't rebook just leave quietly

Research from service industry retention studies consistently shows that the majority of dissatisfied spa and wellness clients don't complain. They simply don't book again. A short survey sent immediately after checkout gives those clients a low-friction way to flag a problem before they've already decided not to return. It also signals that you take quality seriously, which itself builds trust.

Low ambiance scores and low treatment scores need different fixes

If clients rate your therapists highly but score ambiance or welcome poorly, the problem is operations or decor, not training. If consultation scores are low, clients may feel their needs weren't heard before the treatment began. Measuring these separately tells you exactly where to focus. A single "how did you enjoy your visit?" question hides all of it.

Your NPS score tells you which clients are already filling your bookings

Clients who score 9 or 10 on the recommendation question are active advocates. They post reviews, refer friends, and return regularly. Understanding what pushed their experience to a 10 gives you a replicable standard. Clients scoring 6 or below are at real risk of not returning, and the open-text question can tell you why before they've already moved on to a competitor.

Works across spa and wellness categories

The nine questions apply to day spas, massage therapy practices, beauty salons, yoga and Pilates studios, medical aesthetics clinics, and wellness retreats. Adjust the wording for your specific service type, but the structure is deliberately built to move from general impression through specific detail and finish on advocacy intent.
