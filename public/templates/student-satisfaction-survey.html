What This Survey Measures

The Four Pillars of Student Satisfaction

Student academic satisfaction is determined by four distinct pillars — each independently affecting retention, completion rates, and institutional reputation. Strong performance on Teaching while underperforming on Support is a pattern that hits retention for specific student cohorts (first-generation students, international students, students with disabilities) without showing up in aggregate outcome data until it's too late to act.

📚
Teaching
Quality, clarity, and engagement of instruction. Questions 1 and 2.
📝
Assessment
Fairness, feedback quality, and marking clarity. Question 3.
🤝
Support
Accessibility and helpfulness of student services. Question 4.
💬
Student Voice
Whether feedback is heard and acted upon. Question 6.

The Student Voice dimension — Question 6 — is often the most revealing. Institutions where less than 50% of students say feedback is listened to have a structural engagement problem that affects all other dimensions over time. A student who doesn't believe their voice matters stops giving useful feedback in every other context, too.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this student satisfaction survey template free?
Yes. Free forever with no signup from you or your students. Up to 100 responses free. Pro ($19/month) allows 10,000 responses — better for institution-wide surveys covering entire student bodies or multiple cohorts.
What questions should a student satisfaction survey include?
Effective student satisfaction surveys cover: teaching quality, course content relevance, assessment fairness, student support services, learning environment, whether student feedback is acted upon, NPS, return intent, and open feedback. This template covers all nine dimensions aligned with standard academic quality frameworks.
When should universities run student satisfaction surveys?
Run at mid-semester (allowing time to adjust before grades) and at end of year for planning. For continuous monitoring, a 4-5 question pulse per semester is more sustainable than one annual survey. For NSS-regulated institutions, mid-year data gives time to improve before final-year survey submission.
How is this different from the National Student Survey (NSS)?
The NSS is mandatory for UK final-year undergraduates, centrally administered with fixed questions. This template is flexible — run at any time, for any cohort, on your schedule. It captures similar dimensions (teaching, assessment, support, student voice) but gives your institution control over timing and question customisation.
Should student satisfaction surveys be anonymous?
Yes — anonymity is essential for honest academic feedback, particularly about specific lecturers. Students who believe responses are identifiable give significantly more positive and less useful ratings. VoteGenerator stores no names, student IDs, or email addresses by default.
Can I use this for online and distance learning courses?
Yes. Questions 4 and 5 cover support services and learning environment — editable to reference your VLE, online resources, and async support rather than physical campus facilities. The template works for campus, hybrid, and fully online programmes.
How do I use student satisfaction data to improve course quality?
Focus on the gap between highest and lowest dimension scores. If teaching scores 4.3 but assessment fairness scores 2.9, assessment design is your priority. The open text question reveals specific student concerns. Collect at least 20 responses per cohort before drawing conclusions.
Can I use this for professional development courses and bootcamps?
Yes. Works for any structured learning programme — degrees, professional courses, coding bootcamps, corporate training cohorts, and online course platforms. All 9 questions are editable to reflect your specific context.
